library(palmerpenguins)
library(tidyverse)

mu    <- 45.1
sigma <- 5.5

penguins |>
  drop_na(bill_length_mm) |>
  mutate(z = (bill_length_mm - mu) / sigma) |>
  select(species, bill_length_mm, z) |>
  head(8)

3.5.1 Conceptual Questions

  1. The normal distribution is symmetric about its:

    A. Mode only

    B. Median only

    C. Mean, median, and mode (all three are equal)

    D. Variance

  2. Approximately what percentage of scores in a normal distribution fall within ±1 standard deviation of the mean?

    A. 50%

    B. 68%

    C. 95%

    D. 99.7%

  3. Approximately what percentage of scores in a normal distribution fall within ±2 standard deviations of the mean?

    A. 50%

    B. 68%

    C. 95%

    D. 99.7%

  4. In a one-tailed test where the researcher predicts scores will be higher than average, the rejection region is located in:

    A. The lower tail of the distribution

    B. Both tails equally

    C. The upper tail of the distribution

    D. The center of the distribution

  5. The null hypothesis for a z-test typically states:

    A. The sample mean is significantly different from the population mean

    B. There is no difference between the sample mean and the population mean

    C. The sample mean is greater than the population mean

    D. The population standard deviation equals zero

  6. In a two-tailed test at α = .05, the rejection regions occupy:

    A. The upper 5% of the distribution only

    B. The lower 5% of the distribution only

    C. 2.5% in each tail (5% total)

    D. The central 5% of the distribution

  7. The standard error of the mean (SE) is computed as:

    A. \(SE = \sigma \cdot \sqrt{n}\)

    B. \(SE = \dfrac{\sigma}{n}\)

    C. \(SE = \dfrac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\)

    D. \(SE = \dfrac{\sigma^2}{n}\)

  8. When α = .05, the critical z values for a two-tailed test are:

    A. ±1.28

    B. ±1.645

    C. ±1.96

    D. ±2.58

  9. TRUE or FALSE: If the obtained z statistic falls beyond the critical value (into the rejection region), we reject the null hypothesis.

    A. True

    B. False

  10. TRUE or FALSE: A two-tailed test is appropriate when the researcher has a specific directional prediction (e.g., predicts scores will increase).

    A. True

    B. False

3.5.2.2 Part B: z-Test Decision Problems

Assume α = .05 unless otherwise noted. Use the population parameters from the table above.

Problem 5 — Sleep (two-tailed)

A researcher is interested in whether N = 20 patients are getting significantly more or less sleep than average.

  1. Is this a one- or two-tailed test? What is/are the cutoff point(s) at α = .05? (1 point)

 

  1. What is/are the cutoff point(s) at α = .10? (1 point)

 

  1. The sample slept an average of 9.8 hours. State your decision at α = .05 and interpret in a complete sentence; report p < .05 or p > .05. (1 point)

 

 

Problem 6 — Intelligence (one-tailed)

A researcher tests whether N = 100 members of a newly discovered alien species are super intelligent (i.e., score higher than average).

  1. Is this a one- or two-tailed test? What is/are the cutoff point(s) at α = .05? (1 point)

 

  1. What is/are the cutoff point(s) at α = .10? (1 point)

 

  1. The alien sample averages 71 intelligence points. State your decision at α = .05 and interpret; report p < .05 or p > .05. (1 point)

 

 

Problem 7 — Height (one-tailed)

A researcher develops a “shrinking procedure” and measures resulting height for N = 553 participants.

  1. Is this a one- or two-tailed test? What is/are the cutoff point(s) at α = .05? (1 point)

 

  1. What is/are the cutoff point(s) at α = .01? (1 point)

 

  1. The shrunken sample has an average height of 48 inches. State your decision at α = .05 and interpret; report p < .05 or p > .05. (1 point)

 

 

Problem 8 — Memory (two-tailed)

A researcher examines whether a new supplement changes memory performance in N = 1,500 volunteers.

  1. Is this a one- or two-tailed test? What is/are the cutoff point(s) at α = .05? (1 point)

 

  1. What is/are the cutoff point(s) at α = .01? (1 point)

 

  1. After taking the supplement, the sample recalled an average of 23 items. State your decision at α = .05 and interpret; report p < .05 or p > .05. (1 point)
