7.2 Key Formulas

\[F_A = \frac{MS_A}{MS_{within}}, \qquad F_B = \frac{MS_B}{MS_{within}}, \qquad F_{A \times B} = \frac{MS_{A \times B}}{MS_{within}}\]

\[df_A = a - 1, \qquad df_B = b - 1, \qquad df_{A \times B} = df_A \times df_B\]

\[df_{within} = N - (a \times b)\]

7.3 Code Examples

library(palmerpenguins)
library(tidyverse)

penguins |>
  drop_na(flipper_length_mm, sex) |>
  group_by(species, sex) |>

aov(flipper_length_mm ~ species * sex, data = penguins_clean) |> summary()

7.4 Exercises

7.4.1 Conceptual Questions

  1. The main purpose of a factorial ANOVA is to:

    A. Test whether the variance of one sample equals another sample

    B. Test whether three or more group means differ significantly, using variance information

    C. Test the main effects of two or more categorical variables and their interaction, using variance information

    D. Measure the correlation between two variables

  2. A factorial ANOVA requires computing ____, just like a one-way ANOVA:

    A. SS between

    B. SS within

    C. MS between

    D. All of the above

  3. You are comparing how caffeine consumption (Placebo vs. 200 mg) impacts athletic performance (weight lifted), while also accounting for sleep quality (8 hours vs. 4 hours). Different participants are in each condition. This design calls for:

    A. Repeated one-way ANOVAs

    B. Repeated factorial ANOVAs

    C. Pairwise t-tests

    D. Factorial ANOVA

  4. The study described in question 3 is best characterized as a:

    A. 2×2×1 design

    B. 2×2 factorial design

    C. 4-factor design

    D. Mixed factorial design

  5. You are comparing how artistic expression (Drawing vs. Painting) impacts mood (self-reported happiness), while also accounting for diet (1, 2, or 3 meals per day). You find a significant main effect of artistic expression. This means:

    A. The degrees of freedom are significant for that factor

    B. The interaction cannot be statistically significant

    C. The main effect of diet is unlikely to be statistically significant

    D. None of the above

  6. In the study described in question 5, happiness was higher following painting compared to drawing (p < .05), but only when participants ate one meal per day. This finding indicates:

    A. There is a statistically significant interaction

    B. There is a significant main effect of diet

    C. There could be (but is not necessarily) a significant interaction

    D. None of the above

  7. In a factorial ANOVA, under the null hypothesis, the expected value of the F ratio for each main effect and the interaction is approximately:

    A. 0

    B. 1

    C. −1

    D. None of the above

  8. A 2×4 factorial ANOVA yields F(3, 43) = 3.72 for the variable with 4 levels. Assuming α = .05, what should you conclude?

    A. At least one group mean differs in that factor — reject the null

    B. All group means are equal — fail to reject the null

    C. Not enough information to decide

    D. Each sample mean equals zero

  9. You conduct a 2×3 factorial ANOVA. How many degrees of freedom does the interaction have?

    A. 3

    B. 4

    C. 2

    D. Not enough information to decide

  10. A 2×2 factorial ANOVA has within-group degrees of freedom of 24. What is the total sample size?

    A. 30

    B. 28

    C. 25

    D. Not enough information to decide

7.4.2 Computational Questions

Use α = .05 for all problems.

For each problem:

  • Write out the null and alternative hypotheses for each main effect and the interaction
  • Find and report the critical F value for each effect
  • Compute df, MS, and F for each source, showing work
  • Complete the ANOVA summary table
  • Report each result: F(dfeffect, dfwithin) = F-value, p < or > α
  • Interpret each result in plain language

Problem 11 — Factorial ANOVA #1 (2×2; N = 5 per cell; Ntotal = 20)

A researcher examines the combined effects of drug treatment (A: Placebo vs. Active Drug) and therapy type (B: No Therapy vs. Cognitive Therapy) on patient anxiety scores (lower = less anxiety).

Cell Means:

B1: No Therapy B2: Therapy Row Mean
A1: Placebo 10 14 12.0
A2: Drug 8 12 10.0
Column Mean 9 13 11.0
